Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences and improve your browsing experience.

2. How We Use Cookies

LiveTranslateAI uses cookies to:

  • Keep you logged in to your account
  • Remember your language preferences
  • Track usage for billing purposes (free tier limits)
  • Improve our service and fix bugs

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Service to function:

  • Session Cookies: Keep you logged in during your visit
  • Authentication Cookies: Verify your identity (Google OAuth)
  • Security Cookies: Protect against fraud and abuse

3.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance functionality:

  • Preference Cookies: Remember your UI language and settings
  • Usage Cookies: Track translation minutes for free tier limits

3.3 Analytics Cookies

We use Sentry (error tracking) to identify and fix bugs:

  • Sentry Cookies: Help us track errors and improve reliability
  • Data is anonymized and used only for debugging

4. Third-Party Cookies

We use the following third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Google OAuth: For authentication (see Google Privacy Policy)
  • Stripe: For payment processing (see Stripe Privacy Policy)
  • Sentry: For error tracking (see Sentry Privacy Policy)

5. Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy → Cookies

Note: Disabling essential cookies may prevent the Service from working properly.

6. Cookie Retention

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Authentication Cookies: Expire after 30 days of inactivity
  • Preference Cookies: Stored until you clear your browser data

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected in the "Last Updated" date above.
